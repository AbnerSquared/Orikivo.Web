Orikivo is a personal project that originally started as a joke. However, due to my lack of self-control, it has expanded to an ever so thriving system that aims to become a fully dynamic adventure that anyone is free to partake in.
a community gathered.
Orikivo aims to achieve as a dynamic thriving system, from which includes a local area, filled with life, markets, stores, and fellow inhabitants. Feel free to talk to them at any point in time, as they don't bite! (from what I remember) If something feels out of your style, Orikivo is built by your standards. With over 50 detailed options, Orikivo can follow suit by any standards you give it. Block bad words, sites, change how Orikivo shows results, control pixel art rendering, and much more.
fragile, yet always improving.
Orikivo is made by one person. As many know, when development goes this route, there tends to be a mess of underlying bugs and problems that may occur from time to time. Alongside this, there may be commands that could have much easier time working with a couple of changes. This is where the suggestions and report tab falls into play. In the case of you running into a runtime error, or have buggy situations, you are all free to report the bug, from which will be verified/tracked. On the off hand, you can also offer suggestions with how the bot should work specific things, and improvements toward anything else. We all ride together, and it will be worthwhile to those that chip in.
a helping hand.
If Orikivo doesn't suit your fancy with its world dynamic system, you're always free to utilize the toolkit each standard Orikivo is offered with! Calculations, code generations, random sequencers, reminders*, events*, dungeon toolkits*, data management*, moderation*, and more lay foot in your disposal. *These are currently incomplete, and are under construction.
a stylized interface from the retro era.
Orikivo is designed from the ground up in a pixel art style format. If that's your kind of style, you'll enjoy what this bot has to offer! Orikivo focuses entirely on dynamic pixel spritesheets, which allow for fancy font faces, alongside a world of color in an era that seems pretty dim. If certain elements don't follow through with you, you're always free to stick with Orikivo 1.00. :)
